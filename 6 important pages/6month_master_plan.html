// THE 8 RULES — NON-NEGOTIABLE
01Publish something every week. Not when it's perfect. Not after more research. This week. Every week. A bad video published beats a perfect video in your drafts every time.
02One topic at a time. RAG this month. Agents next month. Not RAG + Agents + Fine-tuning + Claude Code + Drones simultaneously. Depth beats breadth for building a real audience.
03Code first, explain second. Never make a video about something you haven't built. The repo is the source of truth. The video is the explanation of the repo.
04Don't build the platform until you have 500 paying students. Gumroad, Maven, or Razorpay + Google Drive works fine until then. Premature platform building kills more courses than bad content.
05LinkedIn daily, YouTube weekly. One LinkedIn post every day (15 minutes). One YouTube video every 5-7 days. This is the minimum. Non-negotiable even during busy weeks.
06No new topic research for at least 3 months. The content plan above is fixed. When you feel the urge to pivot to a new idea, make a video about the current topic instead.
07Failure is content. When something breaks, when your model hallucinates, when your agent loops — document it. These are your best videos. Engineers trust someone who shows what went wrong.
08You are building for 3-year you. In 3 years, when someone hires you, buys your course, or books your workshop — they will find this repo, these videos, these posts. Every piece of content is a permanent asset working for you while you sleep.
